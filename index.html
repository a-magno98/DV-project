The situation now

The start of the global COVID-19 pandemic, and its monitoring on a global scale have created lots of data to be dealt with. What is particularly interesting is that for the first time the general public not only was interested in said data, but also had an easy and mainsteam way to access it.

However this accessibility did not translate correctly to a better understanding of the situation. While this is in part due to how the media portrayed the status of the pandemic by highlighting, every day, the random fluctuations in the number of cases as something that is actually relevant, instead of focusing on the actual trend, part of this misinformation comes form the data itself.
Even when using data from reliable sources it is easy to misinterpret it, either by mistake or maliciously.

For example let's take a look at the following map comparing different countries with their vaccination status and new cases.

Comparing new confirmed cases to vaccination percentage worldwide

December 2021

The data presented here is reliable, and yet it would seem that vaccines do not offer that great of a protection: what's the point of being vaccinated if even the coutries with highest vaccinations rates still get sick?

The problem is that when dealing with complex problems it is difficoult to summarize everything in a single graph, but this is what catches people's attentions and leads to misinformation.

In the following tabs we are going to dive a bit deeper into the current situation and show that, perhaps unsurprisingly, vaccines are indeed working.

The rush for vaccines

The first problem when comparing countries is that we can't boil down everything to a single variable. Elements like the restrictions that are in place all affect the spread of the virus. However when comparing vaccination status one of the most important thing to keep in mind is that not all countries have been able to access the vaccines in the same way.

Overall we can see that the global trend is to get vaccinated, but by looking at the bigger picture we are unable to catch the difference between various countries.

People fully vaccinated with booster dose

If instead we look at how different countries are performing, based on their income class then we can better appreciate the difference among them.
While high income countries are able to afford to vaccinate their population the same cannot be said for lower income ones, that are struggling to reach a sufficient level of immunization.

Keeping this in mind in the next tab we are going to show a better way to compare countries and check that vaccines are indeed working.

Vaccines are working

Now we can go back to the graph shown in the beginning and update it to better represent what is actually going on. Rather than comparing the vaccination status with the number of new cases we can instead look at how vaccines are helping in preventing deaths.

Comparing new deaths to vaccination percentage worldwide

December 2021

Another important element when talking about vaccines is that the pandemic presents a seasonality, and as such one should expect periods where the virus will be less prevalent followed by more difficult months.

When considering all this elements we can observe that vaccines are indeed working, and the average number of deaths in each week is much lower in countries with high vaccination rates.
Of course this is another simplification of a much more complex problem, since we are comparing periods with different level of lockdowns but the deaths in 2021, which we can mark as the start of the vaccination campaing are lower than the same periods in 2020, for countries that have been able to get their population vaccinated.

Average COVID-19 deaths by week

Finally instead of comparing different periods we can check how different people are being affect by the virus.

Here we are focusing on a single week in December 2021 in California: as we can see the majority of cases, but in particular hospitalization and deaths are composed of non-vaccinated individuals. However when looking at the overall composition of the population these are only a minority, with the overwhelming majority of individuals being vaccinated.
